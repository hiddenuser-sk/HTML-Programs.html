The Ramayana is one of the two major ancient Indian epics, the other being the Mahabharata. Traditionally attributed to the sage Valmiki, it is a significant text in Hindu literature and culture.

Summary

The Ramayana narrates the life of Rama, a prince of Ayodhya, and his journey through various trials and adventures. The story begins with Rama’s exile from his kingdom, along with his wife Sita and brother Lakshmana, due to a palace intrigue. During their exile, Sita is kidnapped by Ravana, the demon king of Lanka. The epic follows Rama’s quest to rescue Sita, with the help of allies like Hanuman and the monkey king Sugriva12.

Key Themes

  • Dharma (duty): The Ramayana emphasizes the importance of duty and righteousness.
  • Devotion and loyalty: The characters exhibit deep devotion and loyalty to each other and their duties.
  • Good v.s. Evil: The epic portrays the eternal struggle between good and evil, with Rama representing virtue and Ravana representing vice23.

Cultural Impact

The Ramayana has had a profound influence on Hindu culture and has been retold in various forms across South and Southeast Asia, including adaptations in Cambodia, Indonesia, Thailand, and beyond13. It continues to be a source of moral and spiritual guidance for many.
